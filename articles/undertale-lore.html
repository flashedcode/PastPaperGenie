Undertale Lore Deep-Dive: Monsters, DETERMINATION, and the Human Soul

Unraveling the deep subterranean history of Mount Ebott, the barrier, Flowey’s origin, and the profound moral choices defining Toby Fox’s masterpiece.

Welcome to the ultimate lore and story breakdown for Undertale, available right now in optimized, unblocked browser experiences on PastPaperGenie. Created by indie developer Toby Fox in 2015, Undertale subverted traditional RPG tropes by asking a deceptively simple question: What if you didn't have to destroy anyone? Beneath its charming 8-bit aesthetic and witty humor lies a deeply philosophical narrative touching upon grief, existential dread, memory, and the immense weight of choice.

1. The War and the Barrier

Long ago, two races ruled over Earth: Humans and Monsters. Due to mounting fear regarding the immense magic power possessed by monster souls, a bitter war broke out. The humans emerged victorious, sealing the entire monster population underground beneath Mount Ebott using a powerful magical spell known as The Barrier.

To shatter the barrier and free monsterkind, it requires the combined power of seven human souls. Over centuries, six human children climbed Mount Ebott and fell into the underground realm, only to have their souls captured by Asgore Dreemurr, King of the Underground.

2. DETERMINATION and Alphys' True Lab Experiments

One of the darkest undercurrents of Undertale's lore revolves around DETERMINATION—the power inherent to human souls that allows them to persist after death, manipulate time, and reset timelines.

Royal Scientist Dr. Alphys experimented on extracted human soul fragments, injecting DETERMINATION into fallen monster bodies ("Amalgamates") to see if monster vessels could withstand soul power. Because monster physical bodies are composed mostly of magic rather than physical matter, they melted together into grotesque, tragic hybrid entities trapped within the True Lab.

"In this world, it's kill or BE killed... or perhaps, through immense empathy, you can find another way forward." — Flowey the Flower

3. The Tragedy of Asriel and Chara

Before Frisk falls into the underground, the first fallen human child (Chara) was adopted by King Asgore and Queen Toriel. When Chara tragically fell ill and passed away, Asriel Dreemurr absorbed Chara's human soul, crossing the barrier to return Chara's body to their human village. Mistaken for the murderer, Asriel was mortally wounded by human villagers but refused to fight back, returning to the castle as dust before collapsing onto the golden flower patch in the throne room.

Alphys' subsequent injection of DETERMINATION into that very flower patch birthed Flowey—a soulless, emotionless entity retaining Asriel's memories, capable of saving and resetting timelines at will.

4. The Three Major Routes: Pacifist, Neutral, and Genocide

Your actions in Undertale dictate the entire narrative outcome across three distinct playthrough styles:

  • Neutral Route: The default playthrough where you may choose to fight or spare select monsters, resulting in a fractured underground leadership and lingering questions.
  • True Pacifist Route: By befriending Papyrus, Undyne, Alphys, and sparing every single encounter, you unlock the True Lab secrets and break the barrier for good.
  • Genocide Route: Systematically hunting down and eliminating every monster in every region turns Frisk into an instrument of absolute destruction, confronting the consequences of absolute player power.
