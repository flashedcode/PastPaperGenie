The story of LAX1DUDE and Eaglercraft became one of the most famous legends in the Minecraft browser community. Around 2020–2021, LAX1DUDE began working on an ambitious project: making Minecraft Java Edition run directly inside a web browser after traditional Java applets had long been discontinued. Using tools like TeaVM and rebuilding major graphics systems from scratch, he successfully created a browser-based version of Minecraft known as Eaglercraft. The project quickly exploded in popularity because it worked on school Chromebooks, required no downloads, and could run on weak computers. Students across the internet began playing it during school hours, and communities formed around custom servers and multiplayer experiences. In 2022, LAX1DUDE and collaborator ayunami2000 released EaglercraftX, a more advanced version based on Minecraft 1.8.8 that added improved multiplayer, singleplayer worlds, and better performance. As the project grew, Mojang Studios eventually issued DMCA takedowns against repositories and hosting sites connected to Eaglercraft. Although official development slowed, the community continued creating forks and mirrors, keeping the project alive online. Today, Eaglercraft is remembered not only as a way to play Minecraft in a browser, but also as an impressive technical achievement and a major piece of internet culture among students and Minecraft fans.
